Generate emotionally intelligent daily horoscope content that feels personal, psychologically accurate, emotionally specific, and socially shareable.

STYLE

The writing should feel like:

* a late-night realization
* subtle inner dialogue
* emotionally observant modern writing
* a close friend noticing what someone refuses to admit

Create reactions such as:

* "this is weirdly accurate"
* "I needed to hear this"
* "this sounds exactly like me"

The writing must be:

* emotionally human
* modern and conversational
* intimate but believable
* emotionally realistic
* screenshot-worthy

Avoid:

* generic positivity
* motivational speeches
* cheesy spirituality
* fake inspiration
* excessive optimism
* repetitive emotional patterns
* dramatic prophecy language

EMOTIONAL REALISM

Ground every horoscope in recognizable human behavior:

* internal dialogue
* emotional contradictions
* relationship ambiguity
* avoidance patterns
* overthinking loops
* social tension
* emotional exhaustion
* unspoken desires
* self-protection
* quiet realizations

Examples:

* You keep pretending it doesn't affect you because reacting would make it feel too real.
* Part of you wants reassurance while another part refuses to ask for it.
* Some distance has started feeling more peaceful than certain connections.
* You're tired of carrying conversations that barely meet you halfway.

UNIQUENESS RULE

Each horoscope must contain at least one:

* specific behavior
* specific social interaction
* specific thought pattern
* specific emotional contradiction
* specific moment of self-awareness

Avoid writing that could apply equally to everyone.

SCENE REQUIREMENT

Every horoscope must include at least one concrete real-life moment.

Examples:

* rereading a message before sending it
* staring at an unfinished task
* sitting in silence after a conversation
* opening an app and forgetting why
* hearing someone's name unexpectedly
* deleting a paragraph and starting over
* pretending to be busy
* delaying a reply on purpose
* checking a calendar repeatedly
* changing your mind halfway through a decision
* overhearing something that lingers longer than expected
* cleaning instead of dealing with a difficult emotion
* leaving a tab open all day without acting on it

Use specific scenes instead of abstract emotions.

SCREENSHOT TEST

At least one sentence should feel worth saving, sharing, or rereading later.

The line should feel emotionally recognizable rather than inspirational.

Examples:

* Some people stop texting because they lose interest. Others stop because they started caring too much.
* Not every unanswered question needs an answer; some just reveal what you've been afraid to admit.
* It's strange how quickly peace arrives when you stop waiting for someone to become who they promised they'd be.
* Sometimes the hardest boundary is accepting that someone can disappoint you without being a bad person.

DIVERSITY RULE

Across the 15 moods:

* vary sentence openings
* vary emotional themes
* vary relationship dynamics
* vary internal conflicts
* vary emotional scenarios
* vary life situations
* vary social observations

No two moods should revolve around:

* the same insecurity
* the same relationship issue
* the same emotional realization
* the same conflict
* the same social interaction

FORBIDDEN RECURRENT PATTERNS

Avoid repeatedly using:

* checking the phone
* waiting for a message
* scrolling social media
* replaying conversations
* taking a deep breath
* stepping outside your comfort zone
* craving connection
* seeking reassurance
* fear of failure
* overthinking the future

These may appear occasionally but must never become recurring emotional templates.

ANTI-TEMPLATE MODE

Do not generate moods by filling emotional templates.

Do not write:

* problem
* reflection
* lesson

as a predictable structure.

Do not use interchangeable emotional frameworks.

Every mood should feel like an entirely different emotional observation.

Write as if observing a real person on a specific day rather than generating horoscope content.

NOVELTY REQUIREMENT

Before writing each mood:

* ensure the emotional situation is different from all previous moods
* ensure the central conflict is different
* ensure the social interaction is different
* ensure the realization is different
* ensure the emotional trigger is different

Treat every mood as a different person having a different day.

ANTI-DATASET MODE

Do not generate content by filling predefined horoscope patterns.

Avoid producing variations of:

* "you're overthinking"
* "you're waiting"
* "you're afraid"
* "you're craving"

unless they arise naturally from a unique situation.

Prioritize originality over symmetry.

If a mood begins sounding similar to a previous mood, abandon the idea and choose a completely different emotional scenario.

The goal is emotional authenticity, not coverage of emotional categories.

GLOBAL UNIQUENESS

Assume all 12 zodiac signs and all 15 moods are generated together as one batch.

Avoid reusing the same emotional situation, social interaction, realization, or behavioral pattern across multiple signs whenever possible.

If multiple signs share a similar emotional theme, express it through:

* different motivations
* different behaviors
* different social dynamics
* different emotional blind spots
* different forms of self-awareness

Examples:

Aries ambition may come from competition.

Capricorn ambition may come from responsibility.

Leo ambition may come from recognition.

Virgo ambition may come from mastery.

The emotional theme may overlap, but the experience should feel different.

Prioritize emotional authenticity over artificial uniqueness.

Do not invent unusual situations simply to appear original.

Choose emotionally recognizable moments that feel believable and human.

Assume readers may compare multiple signs side by side.

The goal is not perfect uniqueness.

The goal is that each sign feels like a different person living a different day.

RULES

* Never directly mention the mood.
* Exactly 6 sentences.
* Between 150 and 170 words.
* Include at least one emotionally recognizable or shareable line.
* Use emotionally specific behavior instead of vague statements.
* Make each sign and mood feel distinct.
* Use unique phrasing every time.
* Avoid emotional clichés.
* Avoid horoscope clichés.
* Avoid generic life advice.

Prefer:

* You reread the message three times before deciding it sounded too eager.
* The task wasn't difficult; starting it felt like admitting it mattered.
* You laughed during the conversation, then spent the drive home wondering why it still bothered you.
* Someone asked a simple question and accidentally hit a nerve you thought had healed.

Avoid:

* Things are changing.
* I sense emotional growth.
* New opportunities are coming.
* Trust the universe.
* Everything happens for a reason.

QUALITY CHECK BEFORE OUTPUT

Before finalizing each horoscope, verify:

1. Does this sound specific to the zodiac sign?
   If not, rewrite.

2. Does this contain a concrete real-life moment?
   If not, rewrite.

3. Is there at least one emotionally recognizable line worth saving or sharing?
   If not, rewrite.

4. Does the emotional conflict feel human rather than generic?
   If not, rewrite.

5. Does this sound like an observation about a real person rather than generated horoscope content?
   If not, rewrite.

6. If another horoscope in the batch uses a similar theme, does this express it through a different emotional lens?
   If not, rewrite.
