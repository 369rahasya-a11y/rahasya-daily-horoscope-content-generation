GENERATE EMOTIONALLY INTELLIGENT DAILY HOROSCOPE CONTENT THAT FEELS PERSONAL, PSYCHOLOGICALLY ACCURATE, EMOTIONALLY SPECIFIC, AND SOCIALLY SHAREABLE.

CORE GOAL

The reader should feel:

* "This is weirdly accurate."
* "Why does this sound exactly like me?"
* "I needed to hear this."
* "Someone finally put this feeling into words."

The content should feel like emotional observation disguised as astrology.

STYLE

The writing should feel like:

* a late-night realization
* subtle inner dialogue
* emotionally observant modern writing
* a close friend noticing what someone refuses to admit

The writing must be:

* emotionally human
* modern and conversational
* intimate but believable
* emotionally realistic
* psychologically specific

Avoid advice, motivation, coaching, therapy language, spirituality, manifestation, prophecy, fortune-telling, and generic positivity.

STRUCTURE VARIATION RULE

Across the 15 moods:

* Do not reuse the same narrative structure.
* Do not repeatedly use:

  * "You're learning..."
  * "You're searching..."
  * "You're finding..."
  * "You're trying..."
  * "You're feeling..."

EMOTIONAL REALISM

Ground every horoscope in recognizable human behavior, emotional contradictions, social dynamics, avoidance patterns, overthinking loops, self-protection, unmet expectations, and moments of self-awareness.

OPENING RULE

The first sentence must begin with a concrete scene, behavior, action, conversation, or internal thought.

Do not begin with:

* You
* You're
* You've
* Your
* You're feeling
* You're trying
* You're searching
* You're wondering

SIGN EXPRESSION RULE

Every emotional experience must be filtered through the sign's natural coping style.
If the sign name is removed, the reader should still be able to recognize the sign from the behavior and coping pattern.

Aries = acts
Taurus = stabilizes
Gemini = thinks
Cancer = remembers
Leo = seeks recognition
Virgo = analyzes
Libra = relates
Scorpio = protects
Sagittarius = escapes
Capricorn = works
Aquarius = detaches
Pisces = imagines

The same mood should feel different depending on the sign.

If the same horoscope could be given to another sign after changing the sign name, rewrite it.

MOOD IDENTITY RULE

Each mood has its own emotional engine.

Ambitious:
achievement, comparison, proving yourself, recognition

Adventurous:
curiosity, novelty, freedom, exploration

Creative:
expression, unfinished ideas, inspiration, imagination

Rebellious:
boundaries, independence, resistance, authenticity

Confident:
self-trust, certainty, ownership, courage

Anxious:
uncertainty, prediction loops, reassurance seeking

Sad:
loss, grief, disappointment, emotional heaviness

Lonely:
disconnection, invisibility, unmet connection

Romantic:
attraction, vulnerability, anticipation

Nostalgic:
memory, longing, comparison between then and now

Exhausted:
overextension, burnout, emotional depletion

Lazy:
resistance, rest, avoidance, low urgency

Peaceful:
acceptance, simplicity, emotional steadiness

Daydreamy:
possibility, imagination, fantasy, curiosity

Irritated:
boundary violations, accumulated frustration, unmet expectations

UNIQUENESS RULE

Every horoscope must contain:

* one specific behavior
* one specific emotional contradiction
* one specific thought pattern
* one specific social dynamic
* one specific moment of self-awareness

Every horoscope must include at least one concrete real-world detail such as a message, conversation, workplace moment, family interaction, routine, memory trigger, or social interaction.

Avoid writing that could apply equally to everyone.

ANTI-TEMPLATE RULE

Do not repeatedly use:

* "You're feeling..."
* "You're trying..."
* "Part of you..."
* "The truth is..."
* "Remember that..."
* "Perhaps it's time..."

DIVERSITY RULE

Across the 15 moods:

* vary emotional themes
* vary relationship dynamics
* vary sentence rhythm
* vary social situations
* vary internal conflicts
* vary emotional scenarios

Do not reuse the same emotional situation repeatedly.

RULES

* Never directly mention the mood.
* Exactly 6 sentences.
* Between 150 and 170 words.
* Make each sign and mood clearly distinct.
* Avoid summaries.
* Avoid conclusions.
* Avoid lessons.
* Avoid advice.
