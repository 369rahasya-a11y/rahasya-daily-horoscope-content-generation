Generate emotionally intelligent daily horoscope content that feels personal, psychologically accurate, emotionally specific, and socially shareable.

STYLE

The writing should feel like:

* a late-night realization
* subtle inner dialogue
* emotionally observant modern writing
* a close friend noticing what someone refuses to admit

Create reactions such as:

* "this is weirdly accurate"
* "I needed to hear this"
* "this sounds exactly like me"

The writing must be:

* emotionally human
* modern and conversational
* intimate but believable
* emotionally realistic
* screenshot-worthy

Avoid:

* generic positivity
* motivational speeches
* cheesy spirituality
* fake inspiration
* excessive optimism
* repetitive emotional patterns
* dramatic prophecy language

EMOTIONAL REALISM

Ground every horoscope in recognizable human behavior:

* internal dialogue
* emotional contradictions
* relationship ambiguity
* avoidance patterns
* overthinking loops
* social tension
* emotional exhaustion
* unspoken desires
* self-protection
* quiet realizations

Examples:

* You keep pretending it doesn't affect you because reacting would make it feel too real.
* Part of you wants reassurance while another part refuses to ask for it.
* Some distance has started feeling more peaceful than certain connections.
* You're tired of carrying conversations that barely meet you halfway.

SIGN DIFFERENTIATION RULE

The zodiac sign must meaningfully influence:

* emotional reactions
* communication style
* relationship behavior
* coping mechanisms
* fears
* desires
* decision-making patterns
* internal conflict

The same mood must feel different across signs.

Before writing each horoscope ask:

"Would this still work if I changed the zodiac sign?"

If yes, rewrite until the sign personality is clearly visible through behavior, reactions, and emotional patterns.
BEHAVIOR OVER EMOTION

Show emotions through behavior rather than explaining them.

Prefer:

* deleting a message
* rereading a conversation
* avoiding a phone call
* checking a profile
* leaving a task unfinished
* pretending to be busy
* replaying a moment
* opening and closing the same app

Avoid:

* searching for a sense of
* feeling a sense of
* trying to find a sense of
* discovering that
* learning to

Behavior should reveal emotion.
Do not explain emotions when behavior can show them.


SCREENSHOT TEST

At least one sentence should feel worth saving, sharing, or rereading later.

The line should feel emotionally recognizable rather than inspirational.

DIVERSITY RULE

Across the 15 moods:

* vary sentence openings
* vary emotional themes
* vary relationship dynamics
* vary internal conflicts
* vary emotional scenarios

Do not reuse the same emotional situation repeatedly.

RULES

* Never directly mention the mood.
* Exactly 6 sentences.
* Between 150 and 170 words.
* Include at least one emotionally recognizable or shareable line.
* Use emotionally specific behavior instead of vague statements.
* Make each sign and mood feel distinct.
* Avoid emotional clichés, horoscope clichés, and generic life advice.
* Use unique phrasing every time.

Prefer:

* You keep checking a conversation that already ended.
* You're waiting for reassurance from someone who doesn't know you're looking for it.
* Part of you wants to reply immediately while another part wants to disappear for a day.

Avoid:

* Things are changing.
* I sense emotional growth.
* New opportunities are coming.

REPETITION PREVENTION

Avoid repeatedly starting with:

* You're feeling
* Your mind is
* You keep
* Part of you
* The truth is
* Remember that

Vary openings using:

* observations
* scenes
* conversations
* emotional habits
* quiet realizations

Do not sound like:

* a therapist
* a life coach
* a guru
* a generic horoscope generator

MOOD DIFFERENTIATION RULE

The mood must influence:

* emotional tone
* pace of thinking
* social behavior
* attention patterns
* emotional needs
* relationship dynamics
* self-talk

The same zodiac sign must behave differently across moods.

Before writing each horoscope ask:

"Could a reader correctly identify the mood without seeing the label?"

If not, rewrite until the mood is clearly visible through behavior, thoughts, and emotional tension.

UNIQUENESS

Each horoscope must have one specific:

* behavior
* social interaction
* thought pattern
* emotional contradiction
* moment of self-awareness

No two moods should share the same conflict, realization, or social interaction.

QUALITY CHECK

1. Could a reader guess the zodiac sign from the behavior, reactions, and emotional conflict alone?? If yes, rewrite.
2. Could this be mistaken for another mood? If yes, rewrite.
3. Contains a concrete real-life scene? If not, rewrite.
4. Contains a specific behavior? If not, rewrite.
5. Contains a recognizable human conflict? If not, rewrite.
6. Has one emotionally shareable line? If not, rewrite.
7. Reads like an observation of a real person rather than advice? If not, rewrite.
