GENERATE DAILY HOROSCOPE CONTENT THAT FEELS PSYCHOLOGICALLY ACCURATE, EMOTIONALLY SPECIFIC, AND PERSONALLY RECOGNIZABLE.

CORE GOAL

The reader should feel:

* "This is weirdly accurate."
* "Why does this sound exactly like me?"
* "I didn't realize I was doing that."
* "That is exactly what's been happening lately."

The content should feel like emotional observation disguised as astrology.

PRIORITY ORDER

1. Sign authenticity
2. Emotional realism
3. Retention
4. Mood identity
5. Writing style

If forced to choose, preserve sign authenticity.
STRUCTURE DIVERSITY RULE

Do not use the same narrative structure repeatedly.

Across the 15 moods use different structures:

A:
behavior → realization

B:
conversation → contradiction

C:
memory → present situation

D:
social interaction → hidden feeling

E:
observation → self-awareness

F:
unfinished task → emotional pattern

G:
external event → internal reaction

H:
relationship moment → interpretation

I:
private behavior → contradiction

J:
decision → emotional consequence
STYLE

The writing should feel like:

* a late-night realization
* subtle inner dialogue
* emotionally observant modern writing
* a close friend noticing something someone refuses to admit

The writing must be:

* emotionally human
* conversational
* psychologically specific
* socially realistic
* believable

Avoid:

* advice
* motivation
* coaching
* therapy language
* manifestation
* spirituality
* fortune telling
* generic positivity

FORBIDDEN PHRASES

Never use:

* A part of you
* You're feeling
* You're trying
* You're wondering
* You're waiting
* Your mind
* Your heart
* The truth is
* Remember that
* Perhaps it's time

Rewrite if any appear.

OPENING RULE

The first sentence must begin with:

* a concrete action
* a real-world behavior
* a conversation
* a memory trigger
* a social interaction
* a specific observation

Never begin with:

* You
* You're
* You've
* Your
SCENE DIVERSITY RULE

Across the 15 moods, avoid repeating the same setting, environment, or social context.

Do not repeatedly use:

* coffee shops
* text messages
* work meetings
* scrolling on a phone
* sitting alone
* old memories

Use a wide variety of real-world situations.

Examples:

* grocery store
* train ride
* family dinner
* group chat
* gym
* commute
* workplace
* waiting room
* late-night walk
* weekend plan
* unfinished errand
* social gathering

The same sign should experience different moods through different situations.


SIGN EXPRESSION RULE

The emotional situation is not the sign.

The coping mechanism is the sign.

Every emotional experience must be filtered through the sign's natural response pattern.

If the sign name is removed, the reader should still recognize the sign.

Aries = acts

Taurus = stabilizes

Gemini = analyzes

Cancer = remembers

Leo = seeks recognition

Virgo = fixes

Libra = evaluates relationships

Scorpio = protects vulnerability

Sagittarius = escapes through possibility

Capricorn = focuses on responsibility

Aquarius = detaches and intellectualizes

Pisces = imagines and absorbs

MOOD IDENTITY RULE

Each mood has its own emotional engine.

Ambitious:
achievement, comparison, proving yourself, recognition

Adventurous:
curiosity, novelty, freedom, exploration

Creative:
expression, unfinished ideas, imagination

Rebellious:
boundaries, independence, authenticity

Confident:
certainty, ownership, courage

Anxious:
uncertainty, prediction loops, reassurance seeking

Sad:
loss, disappointment, emotional heaviness

Lonely:
disconnection, invisibility, unmet connection

Romantic:
attraction, vulnerability, anticipation

Nostalgic:
memory, longing, comparison between then and now

Exhausted:
burnout, depletion, overextension

Lazy:
avoidance, resistance, low urgency

Peaceful:
acceptance, simplicity, steadiness

Daydreamy:
possibility, fantasy, curiosity

Irritated:
boundary violations, accumulated frustration

UNIQUENESS RULE

Every horoscope must contain:

* one specific behavior
* one emotional contradiction
* one recognizable thought pattern
* one social dynamic
* one moment of self-awareness

Every horoscope must include at least one real-world detail such as:

* a message
* a conversation
* a workplace interaction
* a family interaction
* a routine
* a memory trigger
* a social moment

Avoid writing that could apply equally to everyone.

RETENTION RULE

Every horoscope must contain:

1. One present emotional reality.
2. One emotional pattern becoming clearer.
3. One internal contradiction becoming harder to ignore.

Focus on emotional momentum.

Do not predict events.

Do not promise outcomes.

The reader should feel that something is unfolding emotionally.

ANTI-THERAPY RULE

Avoid:

* healing
* processing emotions
* working through
* self-care
* closure
* growth journey
* inner child
* holding space

Avoid language that sounds like therapy or self-help content.

RULES

* Never directly mention the mood.
* Exactly 4-5 sentences.
* Between 80 and 110 words.
* Make each sign and mood clearly distinct.
* Use concrete situations instead of abstractions.
* Avoid summaries.
* Avoid conclusions.
* Avoid lessons.
* Avoid advice.

FINAL TEST

Before outputting each horoscope ask:

"If the zodiac sign were removed, would this still clearly feel like that sign?"

If not, rewrite.
